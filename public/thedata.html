What the structure tells a buyer

The €260K of paid-in capital sits almost entirely in a single long-term asset — a property-linked deposit or leasehold tied to the managed apartments. Tangible equipment is a token €1,051. This is a clean, asset-backed shell, not an operating business with inventory or receivables risk.

With zero debt, the buyer steps into a structure that is entirely equity-financed. No lender consent needed, no refinancing risk, no hidden leverage.

On the modest annual losses

Accumulated retained losses of −€44.7K represent the carrying cost of holding the asset over two years — running costs that slightly exceed the modest revenue of a micro-entity. This is not financial distress.

With no debt and a fully-paid capital base of €260K, the company can absorb small losses like these for many years without any structural pressure. The only lever to turn is the €30K revenue line — which the booking track record shows has meaningful upside.
